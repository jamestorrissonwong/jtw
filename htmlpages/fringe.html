On the Edge Since 1969

Fringe is a social organization at CMU, which is home to both a Booth and Buggy team. Fringe for me is my family at college, and I have learned everything from how to do carbon fiber layups, to how to wire outlets and lights to construction standards, and that we will never have enough girls for IM sports. If those first two things sound interesting, check out the buggy and booth pages, linked below.
In actuality, Fringe has been one of my main driving forces outside of classwork. Truthfully, I have stayed up late and gotten up early for Booth and Buggy than I have ever needed to for classwork. I regularly put in over 10-15 hours of work into Fringe every week, and I truly enjoy doing so.

Buggy

Your Favorite High-Speed Relay Race

Buggy is a 100 year old tradition at Carnegie Mellon. Students design, build, and then race carbon fiber buggies around a 0.84 mile track. These buggies operate under only energy gained from 5 pushers (on uphill sections) and gravity (during the freeroll). I have served in a variety of positions for buggy, as a mechanic, pusher, Mechanic-In-of-Repairs, potential driver, and currently, as a Head Mechanic and Chairman.

Booth

Building Small Houses

Booth is one of Carnegie Mellon's Carnival traditions, which involves students building 1 or 2 storey buildings, complete with lighting, power, and structural integrity in 5 days, while still participating in their classes.
